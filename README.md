## Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using SQL, Python, and Power BI. The objective is to identify key factors influencing employee turnover and provide actionable workforce insights through data analysis and interactive dashboards.

The analysis focuses on employee demographics, department-wise attrition, overtime impact, job satisfaction, work-life balance, salary distribution, and other HR-related metrics.

## Key Insights

1. The overall employee attrition rate is 16.12%.

2. Research & Development department records the highest employee attrition among all departments.

3. Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition compared to employees who do not work overtime.

4. Employees aged between 30–40 years contribute the highest number of attrition cases.

5. Single employees exhibit higher attrition compared to married employees.

6. Lower salary bands experience higher employee turnover rates.

7. Certain job roles show consistently higher attrition and may require targeted retention strategies.

---

## Business Recommendations

* Reduce excessive overtime through better workforce planning.
* Improve employee engagement initiatives in high-attrition departments.
* Review compensation and benefits for lower salary bands.
* Implement career growth and promotion programs.
* Strengthen retention efforts for younger and mid-career employees.
* Monitor job roles with high turnover and address underlying causes.
